Double Chocolate Muffins

These muffins are great for a filling morning or afternoon tea. They are fun to make and the ingredients cost $5.00, which is much cheaper and much for fun than buying them already made. The recipe makes 24 muffins so you can give them to friend or even freeze them and take a fresh one out of the freezer each day.

Sifting in the cocoa, after flour and sugar

Ingredients

1 3/4 cups flour
1 teaspoon baking soda
1 cup sugar
2 tablespoons cocoa
100 grams butter
1 egg
1 cup yoghurt
1/2 cup milk
1/2 teaspoon vanilla
24 chocolate buttons

Steps

Turn oven to 200℃

Sift the dry ingredients into a large bowl

Melt the butter, add the remaining ingredients, except the chocolate chips, and mix until smooth.

Add the combined liquids to the dry ingredients and the chocolate chips, then fold together until the flour is dampened but not smooth (i.e. don't overmix)

Place one spoonful of the mixture into a 12 muffin pan (this should be about half the mixture)

Then, place two chocolate buttons into the centre.

Using the remainder of the mixture, cover the buttons, using up all of the mixture.

Bake at 200℃ for 10 to 12 minutes

Cool on a wire rack.
